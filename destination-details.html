Munnar, Kerala

Mist-Kissed Hills & Endless Tea Gardens

Why Visit Munnar?

Munnar is Kerala's crown jewel, famous for its rolling tea plantations, misty mountains, and cool climate. Whether you seek adventure, relaxation, or a romantic escape, Munnar offers breathtaking views, wildlife, and local culture. DiscoverGuide crafts every trip for authentic experiences and lasting memories.

Must-Visit Attractions in Munnar

Experience the best of Kerala's hill station with DreamGuide

Tea Gardens

Walk through endless emerald tea plantations and learn about tea making at local factories.

Eravikulam National Park

Home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr, this park offers stunning views and wildlife spotting.

Mattupetty Dam & Lake

Enjoy boating, picnics, and panoramic views at this scenic dam surrounded by hills.

Top Station

Famous for its breathtaking views of the Western Ghats and Neelakurinji blooms.

Kundala Lake

Pedal boating and shikara rides on a serene lake surrounded by mountains and cherry blossoms.

Echo Point

Shout out and hear your voice bounce back at this fun spot surrounded by lush greenery.

Essential Travel Information

Everything you need to know for your Munnar adventure

Best Time to Visit

September to March for cool weather and clear views. Monsoon (June-August) brings lush greenery and waterfalls.

Weather & Climate

Pleasant year-round. Summer: 15-25°C. Winter: 5-15°C. Occasional mist and rain add to the charm.

Currency & Language

Indian Rupee (INR) is the official currency. Malayalam and English are widely spoken.

Travel Requirements

Indian citizens need valid ID. International travelers require a valid passport and Indian visa.

Local Tips & Cultural Insights

  • Carry warm clothes for chilly mornings and evenings
  • Try local Kerala cuisine and fresh tea
  • Respect wildlife and natural habitats
  • Book safaris and boating in advance during peak season
  • Hire local guides for best experience
  • Support local artisans and eco-friendly tourism
